About Me

I am a second-year PhD student in the Department of Psychology at the University of Toronto, working under the supervision of Dr. Jay Pratt. My research focuses on psychophysical experiemtns elucidating the underlying fundamentals of the inihition of return effect.

I'm also researching the early visual systems with particular focus on the retinal ganglion cells, lateralal geniculate nucleus, and retinotectal pathways. My work bridges cognitive neuroscience and computational modeling to develop biologically-plausible explanations for visual behavior.

In parallel with my experimental work, I develop efficient, parameter-sparse computer vision models inspired by the organizational principles of the early visual system. These models aim to achieve frontier efficiency performance with increased interpretability compared to traditional deep learning approaches.

Research Interests

Visual Pathways

Investigating the distinct roles of magnocellular, parvocellular, and koniocellular pathways in visual processing and attention.

Inhibition of Return

Examining the temporal dynamics of spatial attention and the mechanisms underlying inhibition of return in visual search tasks.

Efficient Vision Models

Developing parameter-sparse computer vision architectures inspired by biological visual processing for efficient AI applications.
